Procensic Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026

About Procensic

Procensic builds a range of AI-powered tools, delivered primarily as Custom GPTs and related digital products (Anki flashcard decks, downloadable guides, and connected web APIs). Procensic's products span several categories, including exam preparation coaches, culinary assistants, quality and engineering support tools, creative/music assistants, and marketing strategy assistants.

This policy applies to all current Procensic products and any future products Procensic releases, organized by category below.

Information We Collect

Procensic does not directly collect, store, sell, or share personal data through this privacy policy page.

When users interact with any Procensic Custom GPT, their conversations are processed by OpenAI according to OpenAI's own privacy policy, data controls, and terms of service.

Some Procensic products connect to backend databases or APIs (for example, to retrieve practice questions, recipes, troubleshooting references, or other reference content). These backend systems are designed to process requests only — such as a topic, category, or item lookup — needed to return relevant content, and do not intentionally store personal user data beyond what's required to operate the service.

Third-Party Services

Depending on the product, Procensic may use third-party services including:

  • OpenAI — Custom GPT hosting and AI processing
  • Cloudflare — API hosting, routing, and infrastructure
  • Supabase — backend database services
  • GitHub Pages — public policy and support pages
  • Lemon Squeezy — payment processing and license key management (exam preparation products)
  • AnkiWeb — flashcard deck distribution and public deck previews (exam preparation products)
  • Suno AI — third-party music generation platform referenced by Suno Copilot

Each third-party service operates under its own privacy policy and terms of service. Procensic is not responsible for the independent privacy practices of those third-party services.
